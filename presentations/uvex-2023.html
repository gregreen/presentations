Advantages of stellar extinction method:

  • Directly measures what we want to know: extinction.
  • 3D extinction: stars trace dust at different distances.

Note: lack of UV photometry in stellar-based dust maps.

UV excess method

At fixed optical color, UV color correlates with metallicity.

UV line blanketing: more metals ⇒ fainter UV.

(Wallerstein & Carlson 1960)

SDSS Tomography

(Ivezić et al. 2008)

Measured [Fe/H] of ~2 million stars using u-g color excess at fixed g-r.

[Fe/H] uncertainty: ~0.2 dex.

Compared to spectroscopy: larger uncertainties, more stars.

NUV vs. u

Precision of metallicity measurement:

$$ \sigma_{\left[\mathrm{Fe/H}\right]} \sim \sigma_{\mathrm{color}} \left( \frac{\mathrm{d}\left(\mathrm{color}\right)}{\mathrm{d}\left[\mathrm{Fe/H}\right]} \right)^{-1} $$

NUV ~6× more sensitive to metallicity than u.

More rigorous formalism: Fisher forecasting.

Fisher forecasting

$$ \frac{1}{\sigma_{\left[\mathrm{Fe/H}\right]}^2} \sim \frac{1}{\sigma_{\mathrm{color}}^2} \left( \frac{\mathrm{d}\left(\mathrm{color}\right)}{\mathrm{d}\left[\mathrm{Fe/H}\right]} \right)^2 $$

$$ \Sigma_{\mathrm{stellar\ params}}^{-1} \sim \left( \frac{\partial\left(\mathrm{colors}\right)}{\partial\left(\mathrm{stellar\ params}\right)} \right)^T \Sigma_{\mathrm{colors}}^{-1} \left( \frac{\partial\left(\mathrm{colors}\right)}{\partial\left(\mathrm{stellar\ params}\right)} \right) $$
