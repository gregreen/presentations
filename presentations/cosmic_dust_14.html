Forward model of stellar spectrum

$$ f_{\lambda}\left(\lambda,\class{star-color}{\Theta,\varpi,E}\right) \class{fragment}{\data{fragment-index=1}{ = {\color{gray} \underbrace{ \color{white} \ F_{\lambda}\left(\lambda,\class{star-color}{\Theta};\class{model-color}{W}\right) }_{ \mathrm{neural\ network} } } }} \class{fragment}{\data{fragment-index=2}{ \, \class{star-color}{\varpi^2} }} \class{fragment}{\data{fragment-index=3}{ \, e^{-\class{star-color}{E} \class{model-color}{R}\left(\lambda\right)} }} $$

Stellar parameters:

  • $\Theta =$ type (LAMOST)
  • $\varpi =$ parallax (Gaia)
  • $E =$ extinction (Bayestar19)

Model parameters:

  • $W =$ neural net weights
  • $R =$ extinction curve

Note: universal extinction curve: $A\left(\lambda\right) = \class{star-color}{E}\,\class{model-color}{R\left(\lambda\right)}$.

Accretion and R(V)

Accretion and R(V)

R(V) tends to decrease.

Occurs at intermediate densities (the translucent interstellar medium).

Large amount of additional mass needed to change R(V) with silicates.

Coagulation and R(V)

Coagulation and R(V)

R(V) tends to increase.

Important in dense cores of clouds.

A simple picture:

  • Accretion reduces R(V) at intermediate densities (steeper exinction curve).
  • Coagulation increases R(V) at high densities (flatter extinction curve).

What about star-forming regions?

  • Cycling of large grains from dense molecular clouds back into the surrounding ISM?
  • Destruction of PAHs by ionizing radiation?

Summary

~130 million R(V) measurements from Gaia XP spectra.

First 3D picture of the extinction curve variation in a large volume of the Milky Way.

Models of dust composition and evolution will have to explain these maps.

Preliminary ideas:

  • R(V) decreases due to accretion (onto PAHs?) at moderate densities, then increases due to coagulation at higher densities.
  • High R(V) in star-forming regions: Destruction of PAHs? Cycling of large grains?
